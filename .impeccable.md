## Design Context

### Users

The primary users are the game designer and anyone playtesting or preparing print materials for the Street Food Night Market party game. They need a fast way to inspect declarative card content, verify that the YAML schema is valid, and preview how the content will translate into printable card layouts.

### Brand Personality

The interface should feel lively, practical, and handmade. It should be clear enough for production work, but still evoke a night market: social, warm, and slightly eclectic.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a print-first editorial direction inspired by market signage, recipe cards, and paper menus. Favor warm paper backgrounds, dark ink, and spice-toned accents. Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, sterile gray shells, and neon/cyberpunk treatment.

### Design Principles

- Content first: the data should be easy to scan before any decorative treatment.
- Print reliability: screen styling must translate cleanly to browser print.
- Tactile warmth: use paper-like surfaces, earthy color accents, and strong contrast.
- Varied rhythm: different content families should feel related but not identical.
- Lightweight structure: the scaffold should remain simple, legible, and easy to extend.
